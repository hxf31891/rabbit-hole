# Japan 26'
***Ordered north -> south THEN east -> west following Shinkasen from Hakodate -> Tokyo -> Kyoto***

## Hakodate

1. Mt. Hakodate Ropeway — One of Japan’s top three night views. Ride up at dusk.
2. Hachiman-Zaka Slope — Photogenic stone-paved slope looking straight down to the harbor bay.

## Aomori

1. Sannai-Maruyama Jomon Site — UNESCO World Heritage site preserving 6,000 years of pre-agricultural civilization.

## Ginzan Onsen

1. Ginzan Onsen Village — Taisho-era hot spring town said to have inspired Spirited Away. Multi-story wooden ryokan lining a lantern-lit river gorge. Bus from Oishida Station runs every 2 hours — plan accordingly. Staying overnight is highly recommended.

## Yamadera

1. Risshaku-ji Temple — 9th-century mountain temple reached via 1,000+ stone steps up a forested cliff. Stunning panoramic valley views. Right on the Yamagata Shinkansen line.

## Sendai

1. Zuihoden Mausoleum — Lavishly ornate mausoleum of Date Masamune set among towering cedar trees.

## Matsushima Bay

1. Matsushima Bay Cruise — One of Japan’s three official “great views” — 260+ pine-forested islands. Take a sightseeing cruise for the full effect. Easy 30-min train from Sendai.

## Tono, Iwate

1. Furusato Folk Village — Japan’s folklore heartland. Open-air museum of preserved farmhouses with staff keeping fires stoked. Feels genuinely remote and time-frozen. Requires a detour off the main Shinkansen line.

## Zao Fox Village 🦊

1. Zao Fox Village — Multiple species of free-roaming foxes in a snowy mountain enclosure. Option to hold a fox for a photo. Closed Wednesdays. Bus from Shiroishi-Zao Shinkansen stop.

## Aizu-Wakamatsu 

1. Tsuruga Castle — Japan’s only castle with red-tiled roofs. Deep samurai history from the Boshin War era. Exceptional in cherry blossom season.

## Nikko

1. Toshogu Shrine — Japan’s most ornate Edo-period shrine, home of the Three Wise Monkeys carving.
2. Kegon Waterfalls — 97-meter waterfall fed by Lake Chuzenji. Take the elevator down for a close-up view.

## Kanazawa

1. Kenroku-en Garden — One of Japan’s three great classical gardens. Stunning in all seasons.
2. Higashi Chaya District — Preserved Edo-era geisha district. Best visited in late afternoon light.

## Ine Fishing Village 

1. Funaya Boat Houses — 230+ traditional boat garages built directly over the water. One of Japan’s most atmospheric hidden gems. Accessible from Amanohashidate on the Sea of Japan coast.

## Hiroshima

1. Peace Memorial Park — UNESCO World Heritage site. Deeply moving — plan at least half a day.
2. Atomic Bomb Dome — Preserved ruins of the 1945 bombing, a powerful symbol of peace.

## Miyajima Island 

1. Itsukushima Shrine & Floating Torii Gate — One of Japan’s most iconic sights. 15-min ferry from Hiroshima.
2. Mt. Misen Hike — Rewarding hike with views across the Inland Sea. Consider staying overnight — the island transforms after the day crowds leave.
